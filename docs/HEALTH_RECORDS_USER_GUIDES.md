## Patient Guide

### Overview
As a patient, you can view your lab results with easy-to-understand explanations, log your vital signs, track your visit history, and stay informed through notifications.

### Accessing Your Health Records

#### 1. Viewing Lab Results

**Step 1:** Navigate to "Lab Results" in your patient portal

**Step 2:** Browse your lab results list
- Recent results appear at the top
- Each card shows:
  - Test date
  - Lab order ID
  - Status (Pending/Completed)
  - Number of tests

**Step 3:** Click on a lab result to view details

**What You'll See:**
- **Patient-Friendly Summary:** Plain language explanation of your results
- **Key Findings:** Important highlights (e.g., "Blood sugar is higher than normal")
- **Detailed Explanation:** More information about what your results mean
- **Recommendations:** Suggested actions (e.g., "Monitor daily", "Follow medication schedule")
- **Next Steps:** What to do next (e.g., "Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks")
- **Test Results Table:** Individual test values with normal ranges

**Understanding Your Results:**
- ‚úÖ Green indicators = Normal range
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Yellow indicators = Slightly abnormal
- ‚ùå Red indicators = Needs attention

**Actions You Can Take:**
- Download PDF report
- Print results
- Share with other healthcare providers (coming soon)

#### 2. Logging Your Vitals

**Step 1:** Navigate to "Log Vitals" in your portal

**Step 2:** Fill in the vitals form
- Blood Pressure (systolic/diastolic)
- Heart Rate (beats per minute)
- Temperature (¬∞C or ¬∞F)
- Weight (kg or lbs)
- Blood Glucose (mg/dL) - optional
- Oxygen Saturation (%) - optional
- Notes - optional

**Step 3:** Click "Save Vitals"

**Important Notes:**
- Log vitals at the same time each day for consistency
- Wait for pharmacist verification (usually within 24 hours)
- Verified vitals appear with a checkmark ‚úì
- You'll receive a notification when your vitals are verified

#### 3. Viewing Your Vitals History

**Step 1:** Navigate to "Vitals History" page

**Step 2:** Review your recorded vitals
- **Chart View:** Visual trends over time
  - Toggle between different metrics (BP, Heart Rate, etc.)
  - Zoom in/out on specific date ranges
  - Hover over points for exact values

- **List View:** Detailed records
  - Timestamp of recording
  - All measured values
  - Verification status
  - Notes you added
  - Pharmacist verification notes (if any)

**Filtering Options:**
- Date range selector
- Show only verified/unverified
- Filter by specific vital type

**Exporting Your Data:**
- Click "Export" button
- Choose format (PDF or CSV)
- Download your vitals history

#### 4. Checking Visit Summaries

**Step 1:** Navigate to "Visit History"

**Step 2:** View your visit summaries
- **Visit Card:** Shows visit date, pharmacist name, visit type
- **Patient Summary:** Easy-to-understand summary of your visit
- **Key Points:** Main topics discussed
- **Next Steps:** What you need to do after the visit

**Step 3:** Click "View Full Details" for complete notes

**Actions Available:**
- Download visit summary (PDF)
- View related lab results (if any)
- View vitals taken during visit

#### 5. Managing Notifications

**Viewing Notifications:**
- Bell icon in top navigation shows unread count
- Click to open notifications panel

**Notification Types:**
- üî¨ **Lab Result Available:** New lab results are ready
- ‚úÖ **Vitals Verified:** Pharmacist reviewed your vitals
- üìã **Visit Summary Ready:** New visit summary available
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Abnormal Result:** Lab result needs attention

**Managing Notifications:**
- Click notification to view related record
- Mark as read/unread
- Delete old notifications
- Configure notification preferences in Settings

**Email Notifications:**
- Enable/disable email alerts in Settings
- Receive important notifications via email
- Customize frequency (immediate/daily digest)

### Best Practices

**For Accurate Vitals Logging:**
1. Use the same device consistently
2. Log at the same time each day
3. Rest for 5 minutes before measuring BP/heart rate
4. Take temperature after 15 minutes of inactivity
5. Add notes if anything unusual (stress, exercise, medication)

**For Lab Results:**
1. Review patient-friendly summary first
2. Read recommendations carefully
3. Follow next steps as advised
4. Contact your pharmacist if you have questions
5. Download copies for your records

**For Visit Summaries:**
1. Review summary within 24 hours of visit
2. Follow all "Next Steps" instructions
3. Schedule follow-up appointments promptly
4. Keep a personal copy of summaries

### Troubleshooting

**"I can't see my lab results"**
- Check if results are marked as "Pending" - wait for completion
- Some results may not be visible until pharmacist adds interpretation
- Contact your pharmacy if results are overdue

**"My vitals aren't being verified"**
- Normal processing time is 24-48 hours
- Check if you filled all required fields
- Ensure values are realistic (system may flag unusual readings)

**"I'm not receiving notifications"**
- Check notification settings in your profile
- Verify email address is correct
- Check spam/junk folder for emails
- Ensure browser notifications are enabled

**"I need help understanding my results"**
- Use the "Contact Pharmacist" feature
- Schedule a consultation appointment
- Call your pharmacy directly

## Pharmacist Guide

### Overview
As a pharmacist, you'll add patient-friendly interpretations to lab results, verify patient-logged vitals, and create visit summaries to improve patient care and engagement.

### Lab Result Interpretations

#### 1. Viewing Pending Lab Results

**Step 1:** Navigate to "Lab Interpretations" dashboard

**Step 2:** Review the pending queue
- Shows all completed lab results without interpretations
- Prioritized by:
  - Abnormal results (red flag)
  - Date (oldest first)
  - Patient status (high-priority patients)

**Step 3:** Click on a lab result to review

**Information Available:**
- Patient demographics
- Complete lab results with reference ranges
- Previous lab results (for comparison)
- Patient medical history summary
- Current medications

#### 2. Adding Patient-Friendly Interpretations

**Step 1:** Review the lab results thoroughly

**Step 2:** Click "Add Interpretation" button

**Step 3:** Fill in the interpretation form

**Summary (Required):**
- Write a 2-3 sentence overview in plain language
- Avoid medical jargon
- Be clear about overall status (normal, attention needed, etc.)
- Example: "Your blood sugar levels are higher than we'd like to see. This means your diabetes needs better management right now."

**Key Findings (Required, 2-5 items):**
- List most important results
- Include the value and what it means
- Use patient-friendly language
- Examples:
  - "Blood glucose: 180 mg/dL (Higher than normal - goal is below 130)"
  - "Cholesterol: 220 mg/dL (Slightly elevated)"

**Detailed Explanation (Optional but Recommended):**
- Provide context for the results
- Explain why values matter
- Connect to patient's health goals
- Address potential concerns

**Recommendations (Required, 2-5 items):**
- Clear, actionable advice
- Examples:
  - "Monitor blood sugar daily before breakfast"
  - "Continue taking Metformin as prescribed"
  - "Reduce sugar intake, especially sugary drinks"

**Next Steps (Required, 1-3 items):**
- Specific actions with timeline
- Examples:
  - "Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks"
  - "Bring blood sugar log to next visit"
  - "Call us if glucose exceeds 250 mg/dL"

**Step 4:** Preview interpretation

**Step 5:** Set visibility
- ‚úÖ "Visible to Patient" - Patient can see immediately
- ‚è∏Ô∏è "Hold for Discussion" - Discuss with patient first before revealing

**Step 6:** Click "Save Interpretation"

#### 3. Managing Visibility

**When to Hold Results:**
- Critical/life-threatening abnormalities
- Cancer diagnoses or screening
- Complex results requiring counseling
- Results that may cause anxiety

**Releasing Held Results:**
- Discuss with patient during visit/call
- Navigate to the interpretation
- Click "Make Visible to Patient"
- Interpretation becomes available immediately

#### 4. Editing Interpretations

**Step 1:** Navigate to existing interpretation

**Step 2:** Click "Edit Interpretation"

**Step 3:** Make changes and save

**Best Practices:**
- Add edit note explaining why you're updating
- Notify patient if significant changes are made
- Keep original recommendations visible in edit history

### Vitals Verification

#### 1. Reviewing Unverified Vitals

**Step 1:** Navigate to "Vitals Verification" dashboard

**Step 2:** View unverified vitals queue
- Sorted by date (oldest first)
- Shows patient name, date/time, values
- Flags for unusual readings:
  - üî¥ Critical values
  - üü° Out of normal range
  - ‚ö™ Normal range

**Step 3:** Filter if needed
- By patient
- By date range
- By verification status
- By alert level

#### 2. Verifying Individual Vitals

**Step 1:** Click on a vital record to review

**Review Checklist:**
- ‚úì Values are realistic (not data entry errors)
- ‚úì Values align with patient's condition
- ‚úì No critical alerts requiring immediate action
- ‚úì Patient notes explain any unusual readings

**Step 2:** Click "Verify" button

**Step 3:** Add verification notes (optional but recommended)
- "Readings normal for patient's condition"
- "BP slightly elevated but patient was anxious - recheck next visit"
- "Temperature spike noted - monitor for infection"

**Step 4:** Confirm verification

**When to Flag Instead of Verify:**
- Critical values requiring immediate intervention
- Impossible values (likely data entry errors)
- Patterns suggesting patient needs intervention

**Flagging Process:**
1. Click "Flag for Review" instead of Verify
2. Select reason (Critical Value / Data Error / Needs Intervention)
3. Add detailed notes
4. Assign to senior pharmacist or physician
5. Contact patient if immediate action needed

#### 3. Bulk Verification

**When to Use:**
- Multiple normal readings from same patient
- Batch of similar readings (e.g., all normal BP checks)

**Step 1:** Select multiple vitals (checkboxes)

**Step 2:** Click "Bulk Verify" button

**Step 3:** Add batch verification notes (optional)

**Step 4:** Confirm

**Important:** Review each value before bulk verifying. Don't bulk verify if any readings are abnormal.

#### 4. Patient Vitals History

**Step 1:** Click patient name from any vital record

**Step 2:** View complete vitals history
- **Timeline View:** Visual representation of trends
- **Table View:** Detailed list of all recordings
- **Statistics:** Average values, ranges, trends

**Using the Chart:**
- Toggle between vital types
- Zoom in on specific date ranges
- Identify trends (improving/worsening)
- Compare verified vs unverified data

**Actions Available:**
- Export patient vitals report (PDF)
- Add manual vital entry (if taken in pharmacy)
- Send message to patient about vitals

### Visit Summaries

#### 1. Creating Patient Summaries

**Step 1:** Navigate to visit record after completing visit notes

**Step 2:** Click "Create Patient Summary" button

**Step 3:** Fill in the summary form

**Summary (Required):**
- 3-5 sentences summarizing the visit
- Use plain language
- Focus on what patient needs to know
- Example: "We discussed your blood pressure management today. Your BP is slightly elevated, so we're adjusting your medication. We also talked about lifestyle changes that can help."

**Key Points (Required, 3-7 items):**
- Main topics discussed
- Important decisions made
- Changes to treatment plan
- Examples:
  - "Blood pressure slightly elevated (145/90)"
  - "Medication dosage increased from 10mg to 15mg"
  - "Discussed importance of reducing salt intake"

**Next Steps (Required, 2-5 items):**
- Clear actions for patient
- Include timelines
- Examples:
  - "Take new medication (15mg) once daily with breakfast"
  - "Monitor blood pressure at home twice daily"
  - "Schedule follow-up appointment in 2 weeks"
  - "Bring blood pressure log to next visit"

**Step 4:** Preview summary

**Step 5:** Set visibility (Visible to Patient / Hold for Now)

**Step 6:** Save summary

#### 2. Best Practices for Summaries

**Writing Effective Summaries:**
- Use second person ("you" / "your")
- Avoid medical jargon
- Be specific with instructions
- Include "why" when possible
- Focus on actions patient needs to take

**Good Examples:**
- ‚úÖ "Your blood sugar is high (180 mg/dL). Take your medication every morning to bring it down."
- ‚úÖ "We changed your blood pressure medicine to a stronger dose because the current one isn't working well enough."

**Avoid:**
- ‚ùå "HbA1c elevated at 7.5% indicating poor glycemic control"
- ‚ùå "Titrate antihypertensive regimen"

**When to Hold Summaries:**
- Awaiting test results before finalizing plan
- Need to discuss sensitive topics more thoroughly
- Coordinating with other healthcare providers

#### 3. Managing Pending Summaries

**Step 1:** Navigate to "Pending Visit Summaries"

**Step 2:** Review list of visits without summaries
- Prioritize recent visits (complete within 24 hours)
- Visits older than 48 hours are highlighted

**Step 3:** Complete summaries in priority order

### Workflow Tips

**Daily Routine:**
1. **Morning:** Review overnight vitals, verify batch if normal
2. **Mid-day:** Add interpretations to completed lab results
3. **After visits:** Create patient summaries immediately
4. **End of day:** Complete any pending summaries

**Quality Assurance:**
- Have colleague review interpretations for critical/complex results
- Use templates for common scenarios (save time while maintaining quality)
- Get patient feedback on interpretation clarity

**Communication:**
- Patient doesn't understand interpretation? Schedule call/visit
- Abnormal result? Call patient proactively
- Follow up on flagged vitals within 24 hours

## Admin Guide

### Overview
As an admin, you manage workspace-level settings for health records features, monitor usage, and ensure the system is configured correctly for your pharmacy.

### Feature Management

#### 1. Enabling Health Records Features

**Step 1:** Navigate to Admin Dashboard > Workspace Settings

**Step 2:** Go to "Health Records Features" section

**Features Available:**
- **Lab Interpretations:** Allow pharmacists to add patient-friendly explanations
- **Vitals Verification:** Enable patient self-logging with pharmacist verification
- **Visit Summaries:** Create patient-facing visit summaries
- **Patient Notifications:** Send alerts for new results and updates

**Step 3:** Toggle features on/off
- Each feature has an enable/disable switch
- Changes take effect immediately
- Consider phased rollout (one feature at a time)

#### 2. Configuring Feature Settings

**Lab Interpretations Settings:**
- **Auto-notify patients:** Send notification when interpretation is added
- **Require verification:** Interpretations need senior pharmacist approval before visible
- **Default visibility:** New interpretations visible to patients by default (or hold)

**Vitals Verification Settings:**
- **Auto-verify normal readings:** System auto-verifies readings in normal range
- **Verification timeframe:** Target time to verify (default: 24 hours)
- **Critical value alerts:** Automatic alerts to pharmacist for critical values
- **Acceptable ranges:** Configure normal ranges for each vital type

**Visit Summaries Settings:**
- **Mandatory summaries:** Require summary for every visit
- **Auto-release:** Automatically make visible to patient (or require manual release)
- **Summary templates:** Create templates for common visit types

**Notification Settings:**
- **Email notifications:** Enable email alerts to patients
- **In-app notifications:** Enable in-app notification panel
- **Notification frequency:** Immediate / Daily digest / Weekly summary
- **SMS notifications:** (if SMS service is configured)

### Usage Monitoring

#### 1. Viewing Health Records Analytics

**Step 1:** Navigate to Admin Dashboard > Analytics > Health Records

**Metrics Available:**

**Lab Interpretations:**

**Vitals Verification:**

- Average verification time
- Verification rate by pharmacist
- Patient compliance rate

**Visit Summaries:**
- Total visits recorded
- % with patient summaries
- Average time to create summary
- Summary completion rate by pharmacist
- Patient views

**Step 2:** Use filters
- Date range
- By pharmacist
- By feature
- By patient segment

**Step 3:** Export reports (PDF/CSV)

#### 2. Quality Monitoring

**Interpretation Quality Metrics:**
- Average interpretation length
- Use of all required fields
- Patient satisfaction ratings (if feedback enabled)
- Edit frequency (indicator of quality issues)

**Verification Accuracy:**
- False positive/negative rate (flagged vs actually problematic)
- Critical value response time
- Patient follow-up completion rate

**Summary Completeness:**
- % including all required sections
- Readability scores
- Patient comprehension feedback
